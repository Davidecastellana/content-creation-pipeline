# Trend Research Methodology

## Research Sources

### Primary Sources
1. **Platform-native tools**: LinkedIn trending, Twitter trending, Instagram explore
2. **Google Trends**: Topic search volume and patterns
3. **Industry publications**: Trade journals, industry news
4. **Social listening tools**: Brand monitoring, conversation tracking

### Secondary Sources
1. **Competitor analysis**: What topics competitors are covering
2. **Content discovery**: What's performing well in your niche
3. **Community forums**: Reddit, Quora, industry discussions
4. **News alerts**: Google Alerts, industry newsletters

---

## Trend Validation Criteria

### Must Validate
- **Momentum**: Is this gaining traction or fading?
- **Source diversity**: 3+ independent sources confirm the trend
- **Brand fit**: Does this align with brand values?
- **Longevity**: Is this a fad or lasting movement?
- **Audience relevance**: Does your audience care about this?

### Trend Scoring
| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | Confirmed momentum, brand fit, multiple sources |
| 7-8 | Strong signals, good fit, some sources |
| 5-6 | Potential but uncertain, partial fit |
| 3-4 | Weak signals, poor fit, or fading |
| 1-2 | Avoid - no momentum or bad fit |

---

## Trend Categories

### Content Format Trends
- Video length preferences (short vs long)
- Carousel vs single image performance
- UGC vs branded content

### Topic Trends
- Industry-specific developments
- Pain points gaining attention
- Cultural moments relevant to audience

### Engagement Trends
- Which formats drive saves vs shares
- Optimal posting times
- Hashtag performance

---

## Research Process

1. **Set scope**: Define industry, audience, platforms
2. **Gather data**: Use sources listed above
3. **Validate**: Cross-reference 3+ sources
4. **Score**: Apply trend scoring matrix
5. **Prioritize**: Select top 5-10 trends
6. **Map to content**: Connect trends to content angles
